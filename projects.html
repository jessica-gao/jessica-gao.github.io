Synergy Theory in Radiobiology


Synergy analysis uses the dose-effect relations (DERs) of mixture components to define a baseline mixture DER characterizing absence of synergy/antagonism. We introduce incremental effect additivity to model the baseline, which has fewer drawbacks than other baseline models, including simple effect additivity. We exemplify incremental effect additivity by discussing the mixed radiation field above low earth orbit, whose components are believed to have highly curvilinear DERs for carcinogenesis.

- Supervised by Professor Rainer Sachs
- Accepted for publication by Radiation Research
- Presented at BaMBA 11. View poster for presenation.

Expiration Analysis of Food Contact Materials


This project analyzes the relationship between the amount of toxic migration from food contact materials and environmental factors, such as temperature, time, and PH measurements. The model can be used to predict the expiration dates of food contact materials.

- Sponsored by General Administration of Quality Supervision, Inspection and Quarantine of the People's Republic of China


AI for Data Science

Artificial Intelligence for Data Science


This project explores new approaches for automated data exploration and insight extraction, while leveraging limited guidance and feedback from human analysts. It attempts to automate the different stages of the data science pipeline, including data wrangling, data cleaning, feature engineering and extraction, model building and architecture search, model criticism and revision, and results presentation and interpretation.

- Supervised by Professor Dawn Song
